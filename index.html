News

EP Released October 11, 2013

Red Dawn’s EP “Antagonist” was released with 5 songs in anticipation of their first self-titled album, expected to be released on November 30. The EP is a provocative, challenging collection of strong lyrics and complex melodies. It’s temporarily available for free download on Noisetrade.

The songs:
  1. Slowly Fading Out
  2. B2B (Back to Basics)
  3. Last Call
  4. Lonely Cosmonaut
  5. Antagonist

Music Video Debut! Live Acoustic Sessions with Dawn

Dawn Kafka was recently featured on WYMS 88.9 Radio Milwaukee. She performed “Antagonist” live on the air. The producers recorded a video of her and posted it to their YouTube channel. Dawn said of the album, “We aim to do more than inspire. We aim to challenge. Our music is written with… much time and intention. Each word is constructed to get across the message that, yeah, life is hard, but we can defeat its attempts to stomp us out. We have a voice that can be heard.”
Radio Milwaukee will regularly be playing “Antagonist” on the air. Be sure to tune in!


Guerrilla Concert a Success

Red Dawn met in a subway and performed “Last Call” on October 4th. Their “guerrilla concerts” - an original concept in which the band tweets hints of location and time of a song performance - had lift off last August, and since then have attracted fans nationwide.
“It’s exciting,” said one fan at the performance on Friday. “There’s a mystery to solve with such an awesome ending. You can’t always solve the puzzle, but there’s an amazing sense of accomplishment and belonging when you do.”

Bio

Red Dawn Steampunk Rock

A revolutionary roar inspiring the people to rise up and rock

Red Dawn began in the motherland. Each member of Red Dawn was born in the former Soviet Union shortly before, or just after, Chernobyl’s nuclear disaster and each dealt with the consequences in different ways. Enduring losses and overcoming them, they each felt a righteous anger and passion for a memorable, victorious life.
In the early 2000s, they all immigrated to Manhattan, New York, USA. Fate brought them together working a blue collar job at a steel factory. Realizing their common roots and mutual love of music, they bonded together. They found a home in the steampunk culture and used their knowledge of metals and music to create custom-made, one-of-a-kind steampunk instruments.
Red Dawn is led by Dawn Kafka, contributing lead vocals and acoustic guitar. Gregor Kozlov is the mind-bending genius behind Red Dawn's electric guitar and writer of many of the band's songs. Bito Buzinsky backs Red Dawn with face-melting percussion. Taryn Mills provides lilting background vocals and a dreamlike upright bass. Dimah Dimitri lays down his sublime voice as backup vocalist. Finally, Danil Olkhovsky is Red Dawn's remixing master and backup percussionist.
Red Dawn’s latest and self-titled first album is a masterpiece of its own species. You have found the official site. Welcome.
Let’s rise up and rock.

Dawn Kafka, guitarist and lead singer

age: 28

Dawn was born in the heart of Chernobyl in a lower class family, days before the disastrous explosion. They quickly moved to St. Petersburg. Despite this, she began work at 9 years old, repairing instruments at her father's shop to help earn money for her family. By the time she was 12, she was building guitars and drum sets from the ground up, and knew how to skillfully play 13 different instruments. At age 15, her family moved to the Americas to start a better life and to receive medical treatment for Dawn not available in Russia. Dawn continued her passion in music, and she became a metalworker at 19 years old to earn a steady income. There, she met the rest of her band, and they found their similarities in struggles and music bonded them on a strong level.

Battling poverty and being an outsider most of her life because of her illness, Dawn finds inspiration in the struggles she once endured. She believes that every person has the opportunity to rise up, but knows they are often wrongfully pushed aside. Through Red Dawn, her goal is to inspire the forgotten to make a name for themselves and be remembered.

Gregor Kozlov, songwriter and electric guitar

age: 22

The thundering electric guitar responsible for laying down Red Dawn’s gritty steam-punk inspired style, Gregor was born and raised in the small city of Kozlovka, Kozlovsky District, Chuvash Republic. Gregor is also responsible for co-writing and co-producing many of the band’s songs. From a young age Gregor always gravitated towards music with a message, with bands like The Clash and The Ramones being a huge influence on him. After graduating from High School, Gregor took a year off to tour Europe and Asia, taking any odd jobs possible and playing music at various clubs and bars on his way. Upon his return to Kozlovka, Gregor made the decision to move to the United States in order to further his education with a focus on art and music. There, he met the rest of his band mates in what could only be described as destiny. The rest is history…

Bito Buzinsky, drummer

age: 31

I was born in Vorkuta of the Komi Republic, which had a population of 70,551 at the time. I was the one. This number has meaning to me since I am an only child. I was born on January 1st 1981, I am 31 years old and I am the only one in my family to leave Russia and live in America. When I was a young boy my father worked as a metal worker in a factory. He taught me everything he knew about metal working, which is why I made my drum set from scrap metal. After moving to America I got a job working as a metal worker, like my father, in a metal factory in Manhattan where I met my band members. We always would talk to each other during breaks and found out that we all played an instrument or could sing. One day after work we decided to get together and play music just for fun. The music sounded great so we decided to become a band. Later, we came up with the name Red Dawn.

Dimah Dimitri, backup vocals

age: 29

Dimah was born in 1984 in Chernobyl, Soviet Union Russia where his family worked at the local iron foundry. When Dimah was at the young age of a year and a half his family decided to move to Manhattan, New York to seek better Iron foundry opportunities, fortunately for them they avoided one of the world’s worst nuclear accidents in history. Growing up Dimah was interested in music and using different objects to make music. In his teen years he fell in love with the rhythm of music and how different harmonious sounds came together to make beautiful music. In high school Dimah was part of the church choir, which made him feel like he was a part of something bigger than himself. After high school he felt a void in his life, wanting again to be a part of something bigger. While working with his family at the iron foundry he met several other individuals trying to start a band but seemed to be missing that rhythm they hoped for. Dimah offered to be part of the band and suggested that a back ground singer would give the band that extra beat they needed. Dimah was a perfect fit for the band which is now on its first tour of the country.

Danil Olkhovsky, mix master and backup percussion

age: 29

I was born November 8th, 1984 in Ufa, Republic of Bashkortostan, Russia. Growing up in Ufa was difficult but rewarding. From a young age, I found myself fascinated with electricity and electronics. However, my family rarely had extra money for technology. To satisfy my curiousity, I learned to take apart and repurpose the things others had thrown away. This would have probably led to a very intersting career in computer repair, unfortunately, due to the Chernobyl incedent I could not find any work locally. Despite this obstacle, I continued following my curiosity. I saw how electronics could be used to make and blend music, and before graduating high school I sucessfully built my first sound mixer. My family and I decided to emigrate to the United States in 2002, hoping for a more secure future. In Manhattan, New York, I took on a job as a metal worker to earn a steady income. After a few years at my job, I met the rest of the band. We all quickly bond with our common past and struggles, and I decided to try my hand at mixing an entire group. I couldn't be happier with the results, and I know you will be too.
